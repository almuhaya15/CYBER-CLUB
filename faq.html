Cybersecurity FAQ

Find answers to commonly asked questions about cybersecurity threats, best practices, and how to protect yourself online.

What is cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity refers to the practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks. These cyberattacks are usually aimed at accessing, changing, or destroying sensitive information, extorting money from users, or interrupting normal business processes.

What is phishing?

Phishing is a type of social engineering attack often used to steal user data, including login credentials and credit card numbers. It occurs when an attacker, masquerading as a trusted entity, dupes a victim into opening an email, instant message, or text message. Our modules provide detailed information on how to identify and prevent phishing.

How can I protect myself from malware?

To protect against malware:

  • Keep your software and operating system up to date.
  • Use reputable antivirus and anti-malware software.
  • Be cautious about opening email attachments or clicking on links from unknown sources.
  • Regularly back up your important data.
  • Avoid downloading software from untrusted websites.

What is social engineering?

Social engineering is the practice of manipulating people into performing actions or divulging confidential information. It relies on human interaction and often involves tricking individuals into breaking normal security procedures. Common techniques include phishing, pretexting, and baiting.

Why is network security important?

Network security is critical because it protects the integrity and usability of network and data. It prevents unauthorized access, misuse, malfunction, modification, destruction, or improper disclosure of network resources and data. Effective network security involves implementing hardware and software solutions like firewalls and intrusion detection systems.

What should I do if I suspect a cyberattack?

If you suspect a cyberattack:

  • Disconnect the affected device from the internet immediately.
  • Do not click on anything or provide any information.
  • Run a full system scan using your antivirus software.
  • Report the incident to your IT department or relevant authorities.
  • Change your passwords, especially for sensitive accounts.
